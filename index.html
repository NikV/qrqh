A new era dawns upon India, as an archaic, pre-colonial ban is finally lifted, making way to a better future for Queering in India for years to come. This date is not just a special day for India, but for the world and all of the Hindu's who have been shamed since the days of India being a colony.

EQUALITY

In Hinduism, Gender Identity and Orientation are not terribly explicit in the original attitudes towards Homoesexuality, but also even to Heterosexuality. Gender Fluidity and Same-Sex encounters have been common amongst different dieties in the Hindu religion. The relationship, of course, is difficult, and for a long time, sexual orientation did not exist, but rather the idea that Men/Women had elements of a "Third Gender", which is elements of both man and women within humans.

While Hinduism hasn't always had a lot of hate or distaste towards Homosexuality, the British Raj greatly influenced the lack of tolerance towards sexual orientation.
