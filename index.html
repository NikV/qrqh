September 6, 2018

On this date, the Colonial-Era law (Section 377) that criminalized homosexual activities was officially revoked, making all activity once again legal in Continental India. From here, a new era can begin with Queering and LGBTQI+ Equality playing potentially a massive role in the future of modern Hindu Society.

More

Equality

After the British began ruling India, many anti-homosexuality sentiments were passed down from European culture and was integrated into Hindu society as a norm up until recently where many started fighting for the opportunity to express their sexuality without the risk of being imprisoned or cast out of their communities. With the revocation of Section 377, even the idea of same-sex marriages can be introduced into Hindu society.


Learn more about the Delhi Queer Pride Parade, a major annual event championing for true LGBTQI+ Equality in Continental India.

Duality, Gender Fluidity, and Third-Gender

Gender Fluidity, Transsexuality, and even androgyny have all been a part of ancient Hindu history that have been seen within the different forms of God. This is also followed by an ancient Hindu belief of the “Third Gender,” which is not male or female, both at once. Furthermore, this supports the emphasis of Duality/Bisexuality that’s found throughout multiple stories in Ancient Hindu text.
